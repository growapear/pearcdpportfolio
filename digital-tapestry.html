Mapping the invisible infrastructures that sustain craft traditions in Bangkok's urban fabric.

The Digital Tapestry is an interactive web project that visualizes the often-invisible ecosystems of traditional craft communities in Bangkok. It maps how knowledge, materials, and relationships flow across spaceâ€”revealing an informal yet resilient network of artisans, workshops, and shared rituals that persist despite increasing urban formalization.

The project emerged from a desire to understand how craft survives when it is unrecognized by formal planning systems. Using site visits, interviews, archival imagery, and spatial analysis, I created a digital interface that allows users to explore hidden communities through interactive maps, sound recordings, and narrative fragments.

Rather than documenting individual makers, The Digital Tapestry focuses on the broader systems that enable knowledge transfer: family ties, spatial proximity, trusted word-of-mouth.

It's not just a research archiveâ€”it's a way of re-seeing the city. The work invites us to zoom into overlooked spaces, listen for ambient histories, and reflect on the fragility and ingenuity of informal systems.

Ultimately, The Digital Tapestry is a foundation: the first step in a longer inquiry into craft as memory, space as archive, and the potential of digital tools to make the hidden visible.

The project began with mapping exercises based on real urban fabric in Bangkok. I conducted fieldwork and secondary research on craft clustersâ€”especially in neighborhoods where knowledge circulates informally, often between houses or across generations.

I used Google Maps API and archival image sourcing to construct a narrative web of spaces. These were translated into an interactive digital map layered with sound, text, and visual cues.

The goal was to move beyond the "maker profile" and focus instead on the atmospheric, spatial, and social conditions that allow craft to survive quietly in the background of the city.

Technical Details

The system utilizes:

Spatial Data Mapping

Manually traced neighborhood geographies and informal craft routes layered onto a digital map

Ambient Sound Integration

Embedded environmental recordings collected in alleyways, workshops, and street corners

Web-based Interactive Interface

Built with Figma and translated into an exploratory website (Figma prototype / HTML available)

Narrative Linking & Metadata Tagging

Each node (person, space, material) is tagged with cultural context and linked to larger systems of support
