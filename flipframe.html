Flipframe

A kinetic object that simulates the rhythm of a day‚Äîmapping memory, motion, and light through mechanical transformation.

Role Designer, Developer, Fabricator
Timeline 1 month
Year 2024
Project GSAPP- Making Senses Course

Flipframe is a kinetic sculpture that explores memory and time through physical motion. Built as part of the Making Senses course, the project draws inspiration from analog display boards and flower-like mechanisms, translating daily rhythms‚Äîlike a sunrise or a user's movement‚Äîinto sculptural flipping actions.

The object functions like a living painting: mounted on a freestanding frame, its tiles flip at programmed intervals, revealing different surfaces and creating a constantly shifting visual narrative. Designed to move slowly and deliberately, Flipframe challenges the pace of digital time by grounding transitions in tactile motion.

The project reconsiders what a display can be‚Äînot something to read, but something to feel. It maps abstract information like time, presence, and routine into mechanical gestures that evoke atmosphere more than data.

Beyond its function, Flipframe becomes a meditation on sensory design: how sound, color, and movement can create meaning without words.

It's not interactive in a conventional sense‚Äîbut deeply responsive, inviting the viewer to pause and reflect with it.

Flipframe evolved from a series of studies in slow movement, mechanical response, and analog feedback. Inspired by 7-segment rack displays and kinetic petals, I prototyped multiple versions using laser-cut gears, 3D-printed arms, and servo motors.

The motion logic was structured around a compressed day cycle (e.g. a 24-hour sunpath simulated in 60 seconds). The tile flipping rhythm mirrors sunrise to sunset across a 10-step cycle.

Material choice‚Äîtranslucent acrylics, PLA, and sound-dampened racks‚Äîwas key to controlling not just motion but acoustic presence.

Technical Details

The system utilizes:

Servo-Controlled Rack Mechanisms

Custom-built rack-and-gear systems rotate individual tiles incrementally, creating a cascading flip motion. Inspired from The Rack Driven 7 Segment Display by engineezy.

Time-Based Motor Programming

Arduino and millis() logic simulate a 24-hour sun cycle using 10 programmed intervals, each triggering a physical transformation.

Physical Interaction Components

Optional sensors (e.g. FSR or PIR) enable motion-triggered responses, allowing the sculpture to react to presence.

Modular Tile Layout

Each tile is laser-cut or 3D-printed and color-coded. Combinations reveal different visual states depending on rotation and sequence.
